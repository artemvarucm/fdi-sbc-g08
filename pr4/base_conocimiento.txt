Like Mercedes-AMG did with the One, Aston Martin poured all their Formula One knowledge, experience, and engineering talent into the Valkyrie, and what resulted is a truly formidable car that looks the part as well. We thought that Ferrari pushed its limits with the LaFerrari, being a hybrid hypercar in the Holy Trinity, but when they turned the LaFerrari into the track-only FXX-K, we were proved wrong. Only 40 units were produced, and all are kept by Ferrari to let their owners drive for track day events, which is a good thing because only Ferrari could keep a 1,036 horsepower car capable of 217 mph truly safe.

Anyone with the privilege of opening the “dihedral synchro-helix” doors of a Koenigsegg knows that something incredibly powerful (and costly) lies inside. The Audi R8 V10 is a 5.2-liter V10 mid-engine supercar that produces up to 620 horsepower. This model is capable of accelerating from 0 to 100 km/h in just 3.1 seconds. Its quattro all-wheel drive and dual-clutch transmission allow for precise and exciting driving. Fuel consumption is approximately 13.8 L/100 km on the highway and 18.5 L/100 km in the city, although its track performance is where it really shines.

The Audi A3 Sportback is a compact and elegant option for those looking for an efficient car but with a premium touch. It comes with engine options that vary from 110 to 190 horsepower, depending on the model and engine type. Its combined fuel consumption is around 6.0 L/100 km, which makes it an economical option for everyday use, without sacrificing comfort and technology.

The Volkswagen Golf GTI is the classic sports hatchback that offers a 2.0-liter turbocharged engine that produces 245 horsepower. Its acceleration from 0 to 100 km/h is completed in just 6.2 seconds. Average consumption is about 7.4 L/100 km, making it an efficient option for those looking for power and practicality. Additionally, it offers precise handling and excellent build quality.

The Volkswagen Passat is a mid-size sedan ideal for those looking for comfort, space and efficiency. Its 1.4-liter TSI engine offers 150 horsepower, while the 2.0 TDI engine offers up to 190 horsepower. With a combined consumption of 5.0 L/100 km for the diesel model, it is a very efficient option for long trips and urban use, with a focus on comfort and safety technologies.

The BMW M4 is the sporty version of the BMW 4 Series sedan, with a 3.0-liter turbocharged engine that produces up to 510 horsepower. It accelerates from 0 to 100 km/h in just 3.8 seconds, offering an incomparable driving experience thanks to its sports suspension and rear (or all-wheel drive, depending on the model). Fuel consumption is around 10.7 L/100 km on the highway and 15 L/100 km in the city.

The Mercedes-Benz AMG GT is a supercar that has a 4.0-liter biturbo V8 engine capable of generating up to 577 horsepower. Accelerating from 0 to 100 km/h in 3.6 seconds, this model offers spectacular performance on both open roads and circuits. Consumption is around 12.0 L/100 km, which is moderate for its power. Its performance-inspired design is equally impressive.

The Porsche 911 Turbo is one of the brand's most iconic sports cars, with a 3.8-liter turbocharged engine that produces 640 horsepower. This version accelerates from 0 to 100 km/h in just 2.7 seconds, thanks to its all-wheel drive and its advanced suspension system. Fuel consumption is approximately 9.0 L/100 km, which, although not extremely efficient, is acceptable given its outstanding performance.

The Porsche Taycan is the brand's first high-performance electric vehicle, with up to 761 horsepower in its Turbo S version. This model is capable of accelerating from 0 to 100 km/h in just 2.8 seconds, offering impressive performance without emissions. Energy consumption is about 23.0 kWh/100 km, which places it among the most efficient of sports electric vehicles. Furthermore, its advanced design and technology make it a futuristic model in all aspects.

The Ferrari 488 GTB is a sports car that is distinguished by its 3.9-liter biturbo V8 engine that produces 670 horsepower. Accelerates from 0 to 100 km/h in just 3.0 seconds. Its consumption is approximately 11.4 L/100 km, which makes it a little more efficient than other Ferraris with larger displacement. Its aerodynamics and handling are exceptional, providing a top-notch driving experience on any type of road.

The Koenigsegg Jesko is a high-performance supercar with up to 1,603 horsepower thanks to its 5.0-liter V8 engine. This model is capable of reaching speeds of over 450 km/h, and its acceleration from 0 to 100 km/h is done in less than 2.8 seconds. Although its consumption is not specifically detailed, it is known to be very high due to its massive power and focus on the track. The advanced technology found in the Jesko, such as the double wishbone suspension system, is what allows it to deliver an impressive and stable ride at high speeds.

The Aston Martin Valkyrie is a hypercar developed with the help of Red Bull Racing, and features a 6.5-liter naturally aspirated V12 engine capable of generating 1,160 horsepower. Its maximum speed is more than 400 km/h, and its acceleration from 0 to 100 km/h is almost instantaneous. Thanks to its extremely lightweight body design, fuel efficiency is not a priority, but it is expected to be around 16.0 L/100 km on the highway.

The McLaren P1 is another high-performance hybrid, with a 3.8-liter twin-turbo V8 engine and an electric motor that combined generate 903 horsepower. It accelerates from 0 to 100 km/h in 2.8 seconds and reaches a top speed of 350 km/h. Consumption is about 14.0 L/100 km, which is not surprising given its focus on power and aerodynamics.
