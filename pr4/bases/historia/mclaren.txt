McLaren, founded in 1963 by Bruce McLaren in Woking, Surrey, is one of the most prestigious names in motorsport and automotive engineering. Initially starting as a racing team, McLaren quickly made its mark in Formula 1 with its innovative designs and engineering prowess. The team’s first major success came in 1968 when Bruce McLaren won the Belgian Grand Prix, and McLaren's legacy has since been defined by its technical excellence and commitment to pushing the boundaries of performance.

McLaren’s Formula 1 team, known as McLaren Racing, has earned a reputation for producing some of the most successful and technologically advanced cars in the sport. Over the years, McLaren has secured multiple Constructors’ Championships and Drivers’ Championships. Notable McLaren drivers include Ayrton Senna, Alain Prost, Lewis Hamilton, and Kimi Räikkönen. Ayrton Senna, in particular, is considered one of the greatest drivers in F1 history, having won three Drivers’ Championships with McLaren between 1988 and 1991.
