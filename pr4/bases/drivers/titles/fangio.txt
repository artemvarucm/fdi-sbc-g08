Juan Manuel Fangio is one of the most legendary drivers in Formula 1 history, with an incredible 5 World Championships to his name, won in 1951, 1954, 1955, 1956, and 1957. Over the course of his career, Fangio secured 24 Grand Prix wins from just 52 races, a remarkable win rate. He also achieved 29 podium finishes. In 1956, he set an extraordinary consistency record by winning 6 out of 7 Grand Prix that season. Fangio was also the first driver to win a World Championship with different teams, having claimed titles with Alfa Romeo, Ferrari, Maserati, and Mercedes-Benz, showcasing his exceptional versatility and skill.
